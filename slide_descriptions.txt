## Introduction: Video Diffusion Models
### Diffusion / Flow-Based Generation
Generate data by learning to reverse a noise process. Flow matching learns a velocity field along a linear interpolation path:
[EQ] $$x_\tau = (1-\tau)\,x_{\text{data}} + \tau\,\epsilon, \quad \epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0,I)$$
[EQ] $$\mathcal{L}_{\text{FM}} = \mathbb{E}_{\tau,\epsilon}\left[\|v_\theta(x_\tau, \tau) - (x_{\text{data}} - \epsilon)\|^2\right]$$
Samples are generated by solving the ODE $dx_\tau/d\tau = v_\theta(x_\tau, \tau)$ from noise to data.
### Spatiotemporal Architecture
Video diffusion models extend image models with **spatiotemporal attention**:
- **3D full attention** (Mochi, MovieGen): joint spatial + temporal tokens
$Q, K, V \in \mathbb{R}^{(h \cdot w \cdot f) \times d}$
- **Factorized attention** (SVD, Open-Sora): separate 2D spatial + 1D temporal layers
- **DiT backbone**: transformer blocks with residual connections + attention + FFN
These spatiotemporal layers are the **core representation** that our thesis modulates.

## Motivation: The Control Problem
### Three Open Challenges
- **Quality vs. Diversity**: CFG improves quality but breaks the learned manifold — reduces diversity, oversaturates, kills motion dynamics
- **Conditional Control**: Adapting pretrained models to new conditions (pose, style, identity) requires large datasets, ControlNet-style modules, and architectural changes
- **Action-Grounded Representations**: Generic video VAEs encode textures and lighting irrelevant to robotics — we need latent spaces that understand *how actions change the world*
### Research Questions
RQ1
How can we modulate the spatiotemporal representations of pretrained video models **at inference time** to improve quality without sacrificing diversity?
RQ2
How can we modulate the temporal structure to enable **versatile conditional control** with minimal data and no architectural modifications?
RQ3
Can we **fundamentally reshape** pretrained video representations — or build new ones — to encode causal, action-driven dynamics for world models?

## Related Work: Sampling Guidance
### Classifier-Free Guidance (CFG)
Ho & Salimans, 2021. Jointly trains conditional and unconditional models, then extrapolates:
[EQ] $$\tilde{\epsilon}_\theta = \epsilon_\theta(x_t|c) + \omega\big(\epsilon_\theta(x_t|c) - \epsilon_\theta(x_t|\varnothing)\big)$$
De facto standard. But the unconditional model is **misaligned** — different task/distribution — causing oversaturation and reduced diversity.
### Autoguidance
Karras et al., 2024. Trains a separate **smaller/weaker model** on the same task and data as the main model. The weak model is aligned — guidance points toward quality, not diversity.
Limitation: requires training an additional model, impractical for large-scale video diffusion.
### Training-Free Alternatives
Self-perturbation methods avoid extra training by creating implicit weak models:
- **SAG** (Hong et al., 2023): blurs high-attention regions
- **PAG** (Ahn et al., 2024): replaces attention map with identity $A \to I$
- **SEG** (Hong, 2024): Gaussian blur on attention weights
All designed for **2D image** models only — no temporal handling.
### Manifold Preservation
- **CFG++** (Chung et al., 2024): modifies sampling rule to reduce off-manifold drift
- **APG** (Sadat et al., 2024): removes tangential component of guidance
- **Characteristic** (Zheng & Lan, 2023): corrective term from Fokker-Planck PDE
>
**Gap:** No unified theoretical framework explains why these heuristics work, and none is designed for video spatiotemporal dynamics.

## Related Work: Conditional Generation & World Models
### Conditional Video Generation
- **ControlNet** (Zhang et al., 2023): external reference network + zero-conv. Powerful but requires large data, high VRAM, spatial alignment
- **IP-Adapter** (Ye et al., 2023): cross-attention injection. Modifies architecture, loses fine details during encoding
- **In-Context Learning** for diffusion (Wang et al., 2024; Hu et al., 2024): spatial grid-based generation. Works for images but grid layouts are unnatural for video
- **Diffusion Forcing** (Chen et al., 2024): heterogeneous noise levels across tokens, enabling flexible generation
>
**Gap:** No method leverages pretrained temporal dynamics for versatile conditioning with minimal data.
### World Models & Representation Learning
- **DreamZero** (2025): autoregressive flow-matching transformer for joint video-action generation. Uses frozen Wan2.1 VAE (14B). Generic latent space not adapted for actions.
- **DINO-WM** (Zhou et al., 2024): pretrained DINOv2 features as world model backbone. Frozen features agnostic to causal manipulation.
- **Unified Latents (UL)** (Heek et al., 2025): variational framework that jointly trains encoder, decoder, and diffusion prior. The prior loss creates an **information bottleneck** that shapes the latent space.
[EQ] $$\mathcal{L}_{\text{UL}} = \underbrace{\mathcal{L}_{\text{prior}}}_{\text{KL}(q(z|x)\|p_\theta(z))} + \underbrace{c_{\text{lf}} \cdot \mathcal{L}_{\text{decoder}}}_{-\log p_\theta(x|z)}$$
>
**Gap:** UL was designed for unconditional image generation. No one has applied it to history-conditioned action-observation dynamics.

## STG: Skip Variants
#### Residual Skip (STG-R)
Standard residual block:
$\text{Res}(z_l) = f_l(z_l) + z_l$
Skipped version passes input directly:
$\text{Res}'(z_l) = z_l$
Nullifies the learned residual $f_l$, reducing representational capacity while preserving the identity path.
#### Attention Skip (STG-A)
Standard self-attention:
$\text{SA}(Q,K,V) = \text{Softmax}\!\left(\frac{QK^T}{\sqrt{d}}\right)V = AV$
Skipped version replaces $A$ with identity:
$\text{SA}'(Q,K,V) = IV$
Removes learned token interactions while preserving value content. Extends PAG to spatiotemporal 3D attention.
### Factorized Attention Models
For models with separate spatial and temporal attention (SVD, Open-Sora), we combine perturbations:
[EQ] $$\tilde{\epsilon}_\theta = \epsilon_\theta + w_s(\epsilon_\theta - \epsilon_\theta^{y_b^s}) + w_t(\epsilon_\theta - \epsilon_\theta^{y_b^t})$$
where $y_b^s$, $y_b^t$ denote spatial and temporal skip perturbations respectively, with independent guidance scales $w_s$, $w_t$.

## STG: Limitations & Extensions
### Limitations
- **Heuristic layer selection**: which layer(s) to skip must be chosen manually per model
- **Extra compute**: requires an additional forward pass for the perturbed model (~2x NFE)
- **Incomplete theoretical justification**: why does layer skipping produce an aligned weak model? No formal guarantee on manifold preservation
- **Fixed guidance scale**: same $w$ applied uniformly — some samples need more guidance than others
>
These limitations motivate **AdaMG**: a theoretical framework that explains *why* STG works and provides principled manifold-preserving guidance.
### Extension: STG Beyond Video Generation
The STG framework generalizes beyond text-to-video:
[ACG: Action Coherence Guidance (ICRA 2026)] Extends STG to **flow-based VLA models** (vision-language-action). The "weak model" removes temporal attention — producing action sequences with more jerkiness. Guidance steers toward temporally coherent, smooth actions.
STG's principle — perturb spatiotemporal layers to create an aligned weak model — applies to **any transformer-based generative model**: images, video, diffusion policies, and VLAs.

## AdaMG: Why Does Guidance Break?
ICML 2026 (under review) · Adaptive Manifold Guidance
### Guidance Violates Probability Conservation
Any guidance field $g_t$ added to the velocity $v_t$ must satisfy the continuity equation to preserve the learned density $p_t$:
[EQ] $$\frac{\partial p_t}{\partial t} + \nabla_x \cdot \big(p_t(v_t + g_t)\big) = 0$$
Since $v_t$ already satisfies continuity, the guidance must satisfy:
[EQ] $$\nabla_x \cdot (p_t \, g_t) = 0$$
Expanding this reveals **two** violation terms:
[EQ] $$\underbrace{p_t \nabla_x \cdot g_t}_{\text{divergence}} + \underbrace{g_t^\top \nabla_x p_t}_{\text{iso-density}} = 0$$
### Two Sources of Off-Manifold Drift
- **Divergence term** $p_t \nabla \cdot g_t$: locally creates or removes probability mass. Empirically **spikes at late timesteps** as samples approach the data manifold.
- **Iso-density term** $g_t^\top \nabla p_t$: transports mass across level sets of $p_t$. This is the primary cause of **saturation and artifacts**.
- CFG and autoguidance (including STG) leave both terms unconstrained — quality improves but at the cost of manifold fidelity.

### STG + AdaMG: One Story

### In-Context Fine-Tuning in Image Domain
In-context LoRA (2024) fine-tunes image diffusion models to generate **collage-style grids** — condition and output arranged spatially in one image.
- Sample efficient:

### Spatial vs. Temporal for Video
Extending in-context FT to video reveals a key empirical finding:
- **Spatial concatenation** (grid layout): video models are **not good** at generating collage-style videos — unnatural for temporal data
- **Temporal concatenation**: video models are **much better** at generating smooth transitions along the time axis — this is what they were trained for
>
**Key observation:** Pretrained video models excel at smooth temporal transitions (abrupt scene changes are filtered out during data curation). We should leverage this inherent ability.
**FIFO-Diffusion** (Kim et al., NeurIPS 2024) showed that diffusion models can stably denoise frames with **heterogeneous noise levels**, even without specific training — motivating our buffer frame design.

## TIC-FT: Training & Inference
### Training
For each pair $(\bar{z}^{(0)}, \hat{z}^{(0)}, c) \sim \mathcal{D}$, sample $t \sim \mathcal{U}\{1,\ldots,T\}$ and $\epsilon \sim \mathcal{N}(0,I)$. Construct the full sequence with buffer noise levels $\tau_b(t) = \min(\tilde{\tau}_b, t)$.
Loss is computed **only on target frames**:
[EQ] $$\mathcal{L} = \frac{1}{K}\sum_{i=L+B+1}^{L+B+K} \|\epsilon_i - \hat{\epsilon}_i\|_2^2$$
Buffer frames are excluded from the loss — the model learns to predict whatever is most natural (typically a smooth fade-out transition), preventing distribution shift.
### Inference
Noise levels at global timestep $t$:
[EQ] $$\mathcal{T}(z^{(t)}) = (\underbrace{0,\ldots,0}_{L},\; \underbrace{\tau_1(t),\ldots,\tau_B(t)}_{B},\; \underbrace{t,\ldots,t}_{K})$$
At each step, only frames at the **maximal noise level** $t$ are denoised. Buffer frames with $\tilde{\tau}_b
- Buffer frames denoise first → provide smooth transition context
- Target frames denoise last → conditioned on clean buffers
- Condition frames stay clean throughout
>
**Why "reasoning tokens":** Buffer frames perform temporal reasoning — they bridge the distributional gap between clean conditions and noisy targets, analogous to chain-of-thought tokens in LLMs that bridge the gap between question and answer.

## AC-UL: The Problem with Generic Latents
Ongoing Research · World Models for Robotics
### Generic VAEs Miss Causal Structure
- **Wan2.1 VAE** (used by DreamZero): retains textures, lighting, background clutter — irrelevant to action dynamics
- **Frozen features** (DINO-WM): agnostic to causal manipulation structure
The autoregressive world model generates:
[EQ] $$p_\theta(a_t, o_{t+1} \mid o_{t-H+1:t}, a_{t-H:t-1}, s_t, l)$$
$a_t$: action, $o_t$: observation, $s_t$: proprioceptive state, $l$: language instruction, $H$: history window
### Our Path: Reshape the Latent Space
Apply the **Unified Latents (UL)** variational objective to fine-tune a pretrained video VAE for action-relevant content.
**Observation encoder** (initialized from Wan2.1 VAE):
[EQ] $$z_{k,\text{clean}} = E_\theta(o_k) \in \mathbb{R}^{h \times w \times c}$$
**Action encoder** (learned from scratch):
[EQ] $$b_{k,\text{clean}} = A_\theta(a_k) \in \mathbb{R}^{d_b}$$
Both encoded into compact latents and denoised with a shared flow matching objective.

## Unified View
Three ways to modulate spatiotemporal representations for controllable video generation
[Guidance]
STG: $\tilde{\epsilon} = \epsilon_\theta + w(\epsilon_\theta - \epsilon_\theta^{\text{skip}})$ — layer skipping creates aligned weak models
AdaMG: $\hat{g}_t = g_t^\perp + \alpha g_t^\parallel$ — manifold-preserving guidance via conservation analysis
(CVPR'25 + ICML'26)
[Reasoning]
TIC-FT: $z^{(t)} = \bar{z}^{(0)} \| \tilde{z}^{(\tilde{\tau})} \| \hat{z}^{(t)}$ — temporal reasoning tokens enable versatile conditional control with 10-30 samples
(NeurIPS'25)
[Reshaping]
AC-UL: $\mathcal{L} = \mathcal{L}_z + \gamma \mathcal{L}_b + \mathcal{L}_x$ — reshapes the VAE latent space via joint action-observation denoising for causal world models
(Ongoing)
>
**Common thread:** All three modulate spatiotemporal representations — via guidance, via reasoning tokens, and by fundamentally reshaping the representation itself.

## Future Directions
#### Adaptive Guidance via Energy-Based Models
Move from fixed guidance scales to **adaptive, sample-dependent guidance**. Use energy-based models as verifiers to dynamically allocate compute — more guidance for harder samples, less for easy ones. Inference-time scaling for video generation.
#### Scalable Conditional Generation
Extend TIC-FT to multi-modal conditions (audio, depth, pose). Explore zero-shot generalization across unseen tasks and scaling laws for reasoning tokens.
#### Hierarchical & Latent World Models
Extend AC-UL to **hierarchical planning**: generate key frames at a high level, then fill in low-level actions. Combine with latent world models (Dreamer-style) for efficient long-horizon planning without predicting every pixel.
#### Spatial Memory for World Models
Current world models hallucinate when revisiting scenes (e.g., turning 360° creates new objects). Add **implicit spatial memory** to store and retrieve observations, enabling consistent long-term generation with persistent environments.

## Summary of Contributions
STG
CVPR 2025
**Spatiotemporal Skip Guidance** — a training-free, plug-and-play guidance method that creates aligned weak models by skipping spatiotemporal layers. Improves quality while preserving diversity and dynamics across Mochi, Open-Sora, and SVD. Extended to VLA models via **ACG** (ICRA 2026).
AdaMG
ICML 2026
**Adaptive Manifold Guidance** — a theoretical framework grounding guidance in probability conservation. Decomposes guidance into divergence and iso-density terms, attenuates off-manifold drift, and unifies prior heuristics (APG, rescaling) as special cases. Zero extra compute.
TIC-FT
NeurIPS 2025
**Temporal In-Context Fine-Tuning** — an architecture-agnostic method that enables versatile conditional video generation (I2V, V2V, style transfer, try-on, action transfer) using only 10–30 training samples. Buffer frames as temporal reasoning tokens bridge condition and target.
AC-UL
Ongoing
**Action-Conditioned Unified Latents** — reshapes pretrained video VAE latent spaces for causal, action-driven dynamics via joint observation-action denoising. A 1.3B model matches or outperforms a 14B DreamZero baseline on real-world manipulation.
>
**Unifying thesis:** All four works modulate spatiotemporal representations of video diffusion models — via guidance, via temporal reasoning tokens, and by fundamentally reshaping the representation — to achieve controllable video generation from quality enhancement to embodied intelligence.
